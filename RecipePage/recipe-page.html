Aunty's Akara!

This recipe will be a simple yet tasty West African recipe for akara (or bean fritters) that will keep people coming back for more! It is typically enjoyed with pap, by itself, or with bread.

Photo Credit:African Vibes

  • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 red onion
  • 1 pack of black-eyed peas (regular or peeled)
  • pinch of salt
  • pinch of black pepper
  • pinch of crayfish powder (can be excluded if you are allergic to shellfish)
  • 2 cups of oil

Ingredient Prep

  1. Soak the black-eyed peas in water for 30 minutes.
  2. Chop the onion and bell pepper in pieces fit for a blender.
  3. Once the beans soften, gently blend to peel.
  4. Once the beans are peeled, rinse them to separate the peel from the bean. Add little water as needed to help blend.
  5. Put the peeled beans in the blender with the bell pepper snd onion.
  6. Blend until smooth.

Cook Time

  1. Heat a sauce pan with the 2 cups of oil, but do not bring it to a boil.
  2. If you want the akara to be fluffy, whisk before pouring 1tbsp of the puree into the oil.
  3. Flip if the akara does not flip itself, fry until golden brown.
  4. Once out of the pan, be sure to coat the plate in paper towel in order to let the excess oil drain.
  5. Can be enjoyed once it has cooled to your liking!
